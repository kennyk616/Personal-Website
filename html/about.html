A little about me

Hi! My name is Chun-Kai Kao, but it's probably easier to just call me Kenny. I graduated from Stanford University with a B.S. in Electrical Engineering in 2012. I am currently pursuing an M.S. in Electrical Engineering, focusing on Human-Computer Interaction and Artificial Intelligence. I will likely take a leave of absence in the 2012-2013 school year to join the Taiwanese army, and return for the 2013-2014 school year to finish my masters degree. In the future I hope to combine my interests in cleantech and software to help make the world a better place. Check out my previous technical and consulting projects!

Experience

I am currently working as a Forward Deployed Engineering Intern at Palantir Technologies. Most of you may not be familiar with this position, so if you want to learn more, check out What is the day-to-day of a Forward Deployed Engineer at Palantir? and What does a Business Development Engineer at Palantir do and how is it different from a software engineer?.

Previously, I have interned at Opower (Arlington VA) as a Product Management and Software Engineering intern in 2011, Facebook (Palo Alto, CA) as a Hardware Engineering intern in 2010, and Hewlett-Packard (Taipei, Taiwan) as a Research & Development Engineering intern in 2009.

Other

I was born in Taipei, Taiwan, lived in Tianjin, China for 5 and 1/2 years, and attended George School, in Newtown, PA, from 10th - 12th grades before coming to California for college. While at Stanford as an undergrad, I was a Project Director for Stanford Consulting (SC), Vice Chair for Green Alliance for Innovative Action (GAIA), held various board positions for Asia-Pacific Student Entrepreneurship Society (ASES), and was a member of the Phi Kappa Psi Fraternity California Beta chapter. I am fluent in Mandarin and English, as well as conversational in Tawianese (Hokkian). I enjoy working out, traveling, and am a huge LA Lakers fan.

Fun fact: I have an asteroid (mini-planet) named after me, called Chunkaikao - technically the full name is 24133 Chunkaikao (1999 VW67) - from being selected as one of the finalists for the Intel Science Talent Search (STS) with my project on fuel cell efficiency once upon a time.
